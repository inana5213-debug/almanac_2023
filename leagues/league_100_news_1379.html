Mets Morishita Joins .400 Club
Sunday, September 11th , 2022
His grandfather once told Yoshinobu Morishita he should set high goals for himself. The Tokyo right fielder has done that throughout his baseball life, beginning as a youngster.

"I hit something like .650 one season when I was a kid," he recalled. "Still, I thought I could have done better."

Now Morishita, who finished 3rd in the HNL in RBI with 23 in 2022, has done what few before him have accomplished: He has become a .400 hitter for a single season. Reflecting on his phenomenal year, with a sly grin, Morishita said, "I think I could have gotten a few more hits."

The Mets star, who finished at .402, collected 35 hits including 8 doubles, 0 triples and 4 home runs. He also had 23 RBIs and scored 19 runs.
